Overview

I built environments, terrain, vehicles, background architecture, props, city layouts, world expansions, event spaces, and branded interactive locations for games and campaigns connected to major entertainment and consumer brands.

That background matters because it taught me how to build for real users under real constraints: performance budgets, client direction, player flow, launch timing, brand approvals, and environments that need to make sense the first second a user lands in them.

Why This Belongs in an Analytics Portfolio

At first glance this looks like creative production. It is also product systems work.

Every high-traffic Roblox world has hidden analytics logic even before a dashboard exists:

  • Spawn placement controls first impressions and early drop-off.
  • Pathing controls whether players understand what to do.
  • Landmarks control orientation.
  • Asset density affects performance and device accessibility.
  • Event spaces need to support crowd behavior, camera angles, social movement, and branded moments.
  • Client IP work requires translating abstract brand goals into interactive layouts users can actually navigate.

That is why this experience fits the rest of my portfolio. My analytics work today is about systems, constraints, bottlenecks, and decisions. Roblox taught me those same instincts through space, scale, and live user behavior.
